The Golden Rules

Voltage Separation

  • Strict Separation: High Voltage (120V+) and Low Voltage (24V/Comms) must never share the same wire duct.
  • Crossing: If they must cross, they connect at a 90-degree angle.
  • Never: Run 120V power for a receptacle in the same panduit as RS-485 comms.

Craftsmanship

  • Ferrules: All stranded wire terminations (24V and Comms) must use ferrules. No stray copper strands.
  • Labeling: Every wire is labeled at BOTH ends within 2 inches of termination.
  • Spare Fuse: Every panel must contain (1) spare fuse taped to the inside door or in a holder.

Cypher Hardware Setup

Edge-X1 Installation

The Edge-X1 is the brain. It must be powered by a dedicated Class 2 transformer. Do not share this transformer with field actuators (valves/dampers).

  • Power: 24VAC or 24VDC.
  • Grounding: Connect the 'G' terminal to earth ground.
  • Network: Use Port 1 (ETH0) for primary uplink.

IO-88 Expansion

Modules slide together on the DIN rail. If spacing is required, use a standard patch cable between the RS-485 ports.

Termination Switch: Only the LAST device in the RS-485 chain should have the EOL (End of Line) switch set to ON.
